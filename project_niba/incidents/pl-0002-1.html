INCIDENT REPORT PL-0002-1 - 7 August 2008
written and compiled by Dr. [REDACTED].

DESCRIPTION OF EVENT
At 13:05, fifteen minutes into the first experiment of PL-0002
(henceforth referred to as Subject), restraints were properly secured
to the Subject. The NIBAD (henceforth referred to as Device) was
powered on and loaded with the experimental compound VORDER-2. At
13:07, the Device was fired into the frontal lobe of the Subject.
The Subject began to scream violently and was thrashing about,
seemingly in an attempt to remove the restraints around their arms
and legs. At 13:09, reporters inside of the testing chamber noted
profuse sweating and bloodshot eyes from the Subject, but made no
comment to Dr. Conners, head researcher of Experiment PL-0002-1.
At 13:12, the Device was powered down, and the observation team was
monitoring the Subject's brain for any sign of change. It was at this
time that assistant researcher Dr. Andrews raised the alarm of the
brain hemorrhage in the Subject's prefrontal cortex. A medical team
was immediately rushed in at 13:13 and took five minutes to
remove the restraints around the Subject. The Subject died to
their injuries at 13:28 in the east medical unit.

CAUSE OF INCIDENT
Many of the researchers on the Experiment PL-0002-1 team have
proposed the theory that the compound used in the NIBAD during the
experiment was improperly proportioned. According to Dr. Conners, he
believes that the compound was too thick, which made the normally
harmless beam produced by the Device do physical damage to the blood
vessels inside the Subject's brain. When questioned, Dr. Andrews
agreed with this theory, but she also added that the NIBAD might
have been miscalibrated or not calibrated at all before the
experiment commenced, leading to an overdose of compound VORDER-2.

OUTCOME OF INCIDENT
The Subject's corpse has been disposed of accordingly following
Protocol 45-A. All members apart of Experiment PL-0002-1 have been
terminated according to Protocol 30-F. Their families have been told
the correct information specified in Protocol 30-F-1. The compound
used in the experiment, VORDER-2, has been recalled and will be
disposed of. More research will be conducted to find the correct
frontal lobe solution for use with the Device. All facilities that
were used during Experiment PL-0002-1 have been properly cleaned in
accordance with Protocol 75-B. Further experiments involving the
frontal lobe have been postponed and all PL subjects have been
reclassified as VS or TS subjects.

END INCIDENT REPORT
